SEVEN WONDERS OF THE WORLD

TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal, built by Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, is a breathtaking white marble mausoleum in Agra. It symbolizes love, featuring intricate carvings, gardens, and a reflecting pool, attracting millions of visitors worldwide.

GREAT WALL OF CHINA

The Great Wall of China stretches over 13,000 miles, built to protect against invasions. Constructed across centuries, it consists of stone, brick, and tamped earth. It winds through mountains, deserts, and plains, standing as a remarkable feat of human engineering.

PETRA

Known as the "Rose City," Petra is an ancient city carved into red sandstone cliffs. Once a thriving trade center, it features magnificent tombs, temples, and the famous Al-Khazneh (The Treasury). Petra remains Jordan’s most iconic archaeological wonder

MACHU PICCHU

Perched high in the Andes Mountains, Machu Picchu is a mysterious Incan city abandoned centuries ago. It features stone-built temples, terraces, and palaces, showcasing advanced engineering. Surrounded by lush green peaks, it is a top destination for travelers.

CHRIST THE REDEEMER

Standing 98 feet tall atop Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Christ the Redeemer is a symbol of peace and faith. Made of reinforced concrete and soapstone, the statue offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city and coastline.

COLOSSEUM

The Colosseum in Rome is an ancient amphitheater, once hosting gladiator fights, chariot races, and public spectacles. Built in AD 80, it could accommodate 50,000 spectators. Despite damage over time, it remains a major symbol of Roman history.

Chichén Itzá

A sacred Mayan pyramid, Chichén Itzá is renowned for its precise astronomical alignment. During the equinox, sunlight creates a serpent-like shadow on the steps. It features temples, a ball court, and an observatory, reflecting the Mayans’ advanced knowledge.
